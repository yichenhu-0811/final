Then, we figured out the combination of population decline and budget cuts has led to the closure or demolition of nearly half of their library branches. In Little Village, there’s no longer quick access to the DPL libraries.

The three closest library branches that still open now are difficult to reach by walking, and it takes a substantial amount of time to get there by bike and bus. Driving remains the most convenient option, but not everyone has access to a vehicle.

On a smaller scale, amenities like benches, Little Free Libraries are achievable, offering a tangible way to empower residents and foster shared ownership in the community. With developments like the Work/Live apartments opening in 2025/2026, Little Village requires social infrastructure to connect current and new residents. Our proposal focuses on creating small-scale projects now to meet this growing need.

We divided the term into two key components: small-scale , defined as something of a limited size and extent. In our case this means t emporary, and modular, so it’s easily adaptable. It is something to be implemented in the short term. Social infrastructure is physical spaces, services, networks, and institutions that support the quality of life in communities.
Our goal is to foster a connected, engaged community where residents feel a sense of ownership, pride, and access to shared learning resources and cultural infrastructure.

Small-scale infrastructure is the first step in encouraging larger investment. To prove that a larger investment is necessary, we start by implementing inexpensive small pieces of infrastructure and gather tangible data on their use .
Through data collection , we can understand what the community wants/needs. In the long term, that data can be used to tailor greater investment and advocate for large-scale infrastructure to ensure long term success.

In addition to the main community hub at the field, there will be additional community boards scattered around the little village.
We envision the community board not just for sharing information, but as an interactive space where community members can participate in its creation, share their thoughts and comments, and foster a deeper sense of engagement and belonging through the process of making.
